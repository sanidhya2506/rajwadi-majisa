Majisa's Family

Majisa and Her Revered Family

Majisa, also known as Rani Bhatiyani, is a legendary figure of Rajasthan, celebrated not only for her courage and devotion but also for her remarkable family. As the eldest among the seven sisters, known as Satho Biosa, she guided her siblings with wisdom and grace. She was the mother of Lal Banna Sa, whose story of loyalty and tragedy is intertwined with hers, and her devotees also honor Sawai Singh Ji, revered as Datha Hukm, who continues to protect and guide those who follow her teachings. Through her family and the devoted figures around her, Majisa’s legacy of strength, compassion, and spiritual guidance continues to inspire generations.

Lal Banna Sa – Son of Majisa

Lal Banna Sa, also known as Lal Singh, was the beloved son of Majisa and Kalyan Singh, the Rathore prince of Jasol. His life was tragically cut short due to the jealousy and treachery of Kalyan Singh's first wife, Devri, who poisoned him. Despite his early demise, Lal Banna Sa remains a central figure in the stories of Majisa, symbolizing loyalty, familial love, and the profound bond between mother and child. Devotees honor his memory, believing that his spirit continues to bless and protect them, highlighting themes of devotion and the enduring connection between generations. His story adds depth to Majisa's legacy and reminds followers of the challenges she faced as a mother, further elevating her spiritual significance.

Satho Biosa – The Seven Sisters

Satho Biosa refers to the seven sisters, among whom Majisa was the eldest. These sisters were devoted to Majisa and played a significant role in preserving and spreading her story, virtues, and spiritual teachings. They symbolize unity, loyalty, and feminine strength, supporting Majisa through trials and sharing her divine message with the community. Their collective presence and dedication to Majisa's cause helped strengthen the cultural and spiritual fabric of Rajasthan. Through rituals, folk songs, and storytelling, the Satho Biosa continue to be remembered as pillars of devotion, emphasizing the importance of family, faith, and shared responsibility in nurturing a legacy that inspires generations of followers.

Sawai Singh Ji – Datha Hukm

Sawai Singh Ji, also revered as Datha Hukm, holds a significant place in the spiritual tradition surrounding Majisa. Considered a source of divine command and blessings, he guides devotees and ensures the continuation of Majisa’s teachings and protection. His presence embodies faith, guidance, and the transmission of spiritual authority, offering followers direction in their daily lives. Devotees seek his blessings and follow his spiritual guidance, believing that adherence to the Datha Hukm brings prosperity, safety, and divine favor. Sawai Singh Ji’s role reinforces the sacred narrative of Majisa, linking her earthly life, her devoted family, and the community’s enduring faith into a unified tradition that thrives even today.
